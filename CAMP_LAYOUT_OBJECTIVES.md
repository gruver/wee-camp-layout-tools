# Camp Layout Objectives And Constraints

This document captures the working assumptions for camp layout optimization. It
should be updated as constraints change, new objects are added, or layout
tradeoffs become clearer.

## Camp Context

- Event: Burning Man 2026.
- Camp address: Delphi & 3:45.
- Approximate site size: 150 ft frontage x 150 ft depth.
- Population target: 25 people.
- Frontage orientation: mid-block facing mountain.
- Street-facing edge: mountain-facing frontage.
- Direction toward The Man: back of camp from the frontage.
- Direction toward mountains: out through the frontage/street side.
- Sun path assumption: east to west across the plan.

## Placement Directive Summary

Burning Man placement states that the frontage is the entrance to camp and
should be treated as the front yard facing the street. Our frontage length and
depth are both approximately 150 ft, and dimensions may be off by a few feet on
arrival.

Because the camp is mid-block facing mountain, standing inside camp and looking
out through the frontage means looking away from The Man and toward the
mountain range. The back of camp is therefore Man-facing.

## Primary Layout Objectives

- Keep frontage welcoming, legible, and not blocked by private storage.
- Preserve a clear 20 ft service lane.
- Keep fuel storage away from kitchen, dense sleeping areas, and major social
  congregation zones.
- Keep water tank access and refill paths open.
- Separate high-traffic public/social zones from quieter sleeping zones.
- Make kitchen access convenient without making it the only circulation path.
- Preserve shade where people will gather during the hottest parts of the day.
- Keep vehicles and trailers placed so arrival/departure and emergency movement
  remain realistic.
- Maintain walkable circulation between sleeping, kitchen, shade, frontage, and
  infrastructure.
- Avoid layouts that depend on exact dimensions, because final playa placement
  can be off by a few feet.

## Required And Preferred Zones

Required:

- Street-facing frontage / entry zone.
- 20 ft wide service lane.
- Kitchen area.
- Main shade / social area.
- Sleeping area for 25 people.
- Vehicle and trailer parking.
- Fuel storage.
- Water storage and water-tank access keepout.
- Trash, gray water, or utility staging if needed.

Preferred:

- Bike parking close to frontage but not blocking the entry.
- Lounge furniture under shade.
- Clear route from frontage to kitchen and main shade.
- Quiet sleeping zone deeper in camp or shielded by vehicles/shade.
- Infrastructure zones at edges when practical.

## Access Constraints

- The service lane must remain clear across its full width.
- Water storage must have a keepout/access path large enough for refill access.
- Fuel storage should remain visible and reachable but away from ignition and
  food-prep areas.
- Trailers and large vans should be positioned with realistic turning and access
  assumptions.
- Do not block the frontage with a continuous wall of vehicles unless that is an
  explicit design choice.

## Direction And Shade Assumptions

- Frontage/street side faces the mountains.
- Back of camp points toward The Man.
- Sun travel is treated as east to west for shade planning.
- Standard shade structures are mostly Black Rock shade modules, assumed to be
  12 ft x 20 ft each.
- Shade structures should prioritize kitchen, lounge, and high-use daytime
  areas.
- Sleeping areas benefit from morning/afternoon shade but should not consume all
  public shade resources.

## People And Capacity Assumptions

- Planning population: 25 people.
- Sleeping layout should support enough tents, vans, trailers, or shared
  sleeping structures for the group.
- Common infrastructure should assume repeated daily use by 25 people.
- Circulation paths should support people carrying water, food bins, bikes,
  shade hardware, and trash without threading through private sleeping areas.

## Open Questions

- Exact vehicle, trailer, and van count.
- Exact kitchen footprint and fire/heat-producing equipment.
- Water tank dimensions and refill access requirements.
- Fuel storage volume and safety distance targets.
- Number and size of shade structures.
- Tent count versus vehicle/trailer sleepers.
- Whether frontage should include a public-facing activation, bar, lounge, or
  art element.
- Whether the 20 ft service lane must run front-to-back, side-to-side, or along a
  specific edge.
- Whether there are known neighbor, road, or survey constraints once on playa.

## Layout Review Checklist

- Does the plan preserve the 20 ft service lane?
- Is frontage welcoming and not overrun by storage?
- Can water be refilled without moving major objects?
- Is fuel separated from kitchen and dense sleeping areas?
- Are kitchen, shade, and sleeping zones connected by clear paths?
- Are large vehicles and trailers realistically placed?
- Does the layout still work if site dimensions shift by a few feet?
- Are sun and shade assumptions visible in the exported plan?
- Does the inventory match the current design assumptions?
